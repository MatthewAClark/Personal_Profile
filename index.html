Personal Rail Passenger App

After using many apps for planning my rail journeys, I realised there was not one app that tailoured journeys towards the indidual's needs for regularly travel between their stations of use. Not only this, but most apps out there did not display what services were regularly delayed so that users could plan their journey's around expected late trains. I therefore decided to design an app where the user's specific train routes and destinations could be stored within the app, along with popular schedules that they use. The user is then able to look at the app's home screen which details departures happening now from their favourite stations and whether they are late. Train punctualilty is also included historically in another section, so that users can expect delays from certain trains on certain schedules.

view API

Technical Details

It became apparent early on that a relational database would be the best approach regarding this data, since train stations can relate to train schedules and train schedules can relate to their performance data. Since I had learnt PostgreSQL on my course at Northcoders, I decided to use that technology as I had the support and understanding through my studies.

The database contains 4 tables, stations, routes, schedules and performances. By splitting the data up in this way has made it easier to work with specific data sets that are related by one common key.
