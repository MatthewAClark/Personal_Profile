Personal Train Travel App

This is an app that allows rail journeys to be monitored so that the end user can plan their journeys and navigate around services that are expected to be delayed. The app was created with the idea of making journey planning a more personalised experience for the traveller on routes that they regularly travel on, for example commuting between home and work.

Train services along specific routes are stored within a database where they are checked daily for status updates. The updates are then recorded into the database where they can be retrieved from the front end and displayed as percentages reliability against routes or train operating companies.

Technical Details

It became apparent early on that a relational database would be the best approach regarding this data, since train stations can relate to train schedules and train schedules can relate to their performance data. Since I had learnt PostgreSQL on my course at Northcoders, I decided to use that technology as I had the support and understanding through my studies.

The database contains 4 tables, stations, routes, schedules and performances. By splitting the data up in this way has made it easier to work with specific data sets that are related by one common key.
